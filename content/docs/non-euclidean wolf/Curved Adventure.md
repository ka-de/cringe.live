# Curved Adventure

In the kingdom of Lyrasol, where the laws of geometry bend to nature's whims, lived Kade, a white wolf gifted with arcane powers. His world was a tapestry of non-Euclidean spaces, where distances were measured not by straight lines, but by curves dictated by a mysterious parameter, {{< katex >}}p{{< /katex >}}.

As Kade set out on a quest to retrieve a powerful artifact, he found himself traversing landscapes where the Pythagorean theorem had lost its dominion. The distance {{< katex >}}d{{< /katex >}} between two points {{< katex >}}(x_1, y_1){{< /katex >}} and {{< katex >}}(x_2, y_2){{< /katex >}} was given by the enigmatic formula:

{{< katex display=true >}}
d = \sqrt{|x_2 - x_1|^p + |y_2 - y_1|^p}
{{< /katex >}}

With each step, Kade felt the fabric of space-time bend around him, challenging his very understanding of geometry.

To navigate this twisted realm, Kade delved into the mysteries of non-Euclidean linear algebra. He learned that even the fundamental operations, such as the dot product between two vectors {{< katex >}}v{{< /katex >}} and {{< katex >}}w{{< /katex >}}, took on a new form:

{{< katex display=true >}}
v \cdot w = \sum_{i=1}^{n} v_i^p w_i^p
{{< /katex >}}

Where {{< katex >}}v_i{{< /katex >}} and {{< katex >}}w_i{{< /katex >}} were the components of the vectors, raised to the power of {{< katex >}}p{{< /katex >}}, the curvature parameter that governed this strange land.

As Kade journeyed deeper into the non-Euclidean wilderness, he encountered ancient tomes that spoke of a powerful technique called "low-rank adaptation." He discovered that any matrix {{< katex >}}A{{< /katex >}} of rank {{< katex >}}r{{< /katex >}} could be approximated by a matrix {{< katex >}}\tilde{A}{{< /katex >}} of lower rank {{< katex >}}k < r{{< /katex >}} through a process called singular value decomposition (SVD):

{{< katex display=true >}}
A = U \Sigma V^T
{{< /katex >}}

Where {{< katex >}}U{{< /katex >}} and {{< katex >}}V{{< /katex >}} were orthogonal matrices, and {{< katex >}}\Sigma{{< /katex >}} was a diagonal matrix containing the singular values of {{< katex >}}A{{< /katex >}}.

With this knowledge, Kade could adapt to the ever-changing landscapes, bending the rules of linear algebra to his will, as he ventured closer to his goal.
